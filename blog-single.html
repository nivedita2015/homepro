Q and A

Why are new homes so expensive in Boston?

Jan 232016
Jennifer
General

Description:
It looks to me like the cost of a tear down in Boston is about $300k - $400k inside the 95 belt (Lexington, Arlington, and Newton for example). New ~2k - 2500sf homes in those areas sell for about $1.2mil but the cost building those homes looks to be about $300k - $400k. Construction costs based on sampling on Homewyse don't appear to be higher than the rest of the country. What accounts for the difference between selling prices (~$1.2mil) and perceived cost then (~$800k)?

2 Answers
  • Hamad Ali
    Commented on 01/26/2016

    It is very expensive and time consuming to get permits etc. in the Boston area. Cities and towns don't like to see teardowns taking place and you can get community opposition. Also, a tear down for $300 in the towns you cited - not likely. Think $500K minimum. Construction costs here are higher, labor is expensive. It is expensive doing construction around Boston because you have restrictions on site conditions and nuisance complaints during construction. Everything takes longer, and you are carrying the costs meanwhile. There is high demand in blue chip cities like Newton and Cambridge but you have to know what you re doing.
